1. PROBLEM STATEMENT
   1.1 Current Challenges
   For Students with Disabilities:
   • Existing university systems lack comprehensive accessibility features
   • Course materials often unavailable in accessible formats
   • Limited communication options create barriers to participation
   • Technology barriers reduce independence and academic performance
   • Students must request individual accommodations, causing delays
   For Faculty:
   • Lack of tools to create accessible content easily
   • Difficulty communicating with students using different modalities
   • Time-consuming manual accessibility conversions
   • Limited understanding of diverse accessibility needs
   For University:
   • Risk of non-compliance with accessibility regulations (ADA, Section 508)
   • Potential legal liability for inadequate accommodations
   • Missed opportunities for diverse enrollment
   • Reputation impact from accessibility complaints

---

2. PROPOSED SOLUTION
   2.1 Platform Overview
   The Babcock University BDD Inclusive Platform is a comprehensive web-based system that provides:
   Core Features:
1. Multi-Modal Communication Hub
   o Text-based chat with screen reader optimization
   o Symbol/icon communication board (AAC)
   o Video with sign language interpreter support
   o Voice-to-text transcription
   o Email with multiple format options
   o Braille display device connectivity
1. Accessible Content Delivery
   o Automatic caption generation for videos
   o Text transcripts for all audio content
   o Document conversion to accessible formats
   o Audio descriptions for visual content
   o Alt-text for images
   o Downloadable materials in multiple formats
1. Course Management System
   o Accessible assignment submission
   o Screen reader-optimized quizzes/exams
   o Calendar with multiple reminder types
   o Grade viewing with audio feedback
   o Discussion forums with threading support
1. Resource Library
   o Accessibility tutorials and guides
   o Assistive technology configuration profiles
   o Sign language video dictionary
   o Symbol libraries for communication
   o Help documentation in multiple formats
1. Personalization & Preferences
   o Custom text size and contrast settings
   o Preferred communication method storage
   o Assistive technology compatibility profiles
   o Cross-device preference synchronization
   2.2 Technical Approach
   Architecture:
   • Modern web technologies (React.js, Node.js)
   • Cloud-hosted for reliability and scalability (Firebase Hosting / Firebase Services)
   • RESTful API for integration flexibility
   • Responsive design for all devices
   • Progressive Web App (PWA) capabilities
   Accessibility Standards:
   • WCAG 2.1 Level AAA compliance
   • Section 508 conformance
   • Tested with JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver screen readers
   • Full keyboard navigation support
   • High contrast and text scaling
   Security:
   • End-to-end encryption for all communications
1. PROPOSAL OBJECTIVES
   This proposal aims to achieve the following objectives:
   Primary Objectives:
   • Ensure Legal Compliance - Achieve full compliance with ADA, Section 508, and FERPA regulations to eliminate risk of accessibility lawsuits
   • Provide Equitable Access - Enable all students with disabilities to access course materials and participate fully without accommodation delays
   • Reduce Operational Costs - Decrease reactive accommodation expenses by implementing proactive accessibility solutions
   Secondary Objectives:
   • Enhance University Reputation - Position Babcock as a leader in inclusive education technology
   • Improve Student Outcomes - Increase retention and graduation rates for students with disabilities
   • Empower Faculty - Provide tools for creating accessible content efficiently
   • Increase Diversity - Attract and retain diverse student enrollment

7. RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION
   For University:
   • Risk of non-compliance with the Discrimination Against Persons with Disabilities (Prohibition) Act, 2018
   • Potential legal liability and fines (up to ₦100,000) for inadequate accommodations
   • Missed opportunities for diverse enrollment
   • Reputation impact from accessibility complaints
   • High cost of reactive accommodations vs. proactive solutions 1.2 Impact Statistics
   • 15-20% of university-age individuals have some form of disability (WHO)
   • 67% of students with disabilities report technology barriers in education
   • Nigerian institutions face penalties of up to ₦100,000 for non-compliance with disability rights legislation
   • 38% higher dropout rates among students with disabilities due to lack of support
   • $2.3 billion annual market for accessible education technology
   7.1 Technical Risks
   Risk: Inadequate Accessibility Compliance
   • Impact: High - Legal liability, user dissatisfaction
   • Likelihood: Medium
   • Mitigation:
   o Hire certified accessibility consultant from project start
   o Conduct testing with actual BDD users throughout development
   Risk: Integration Challenges with Existing Systems
   • Impact: Medium - Delays, additional costs
   • Likelihood: Medium
   • Mitigation:
   o Conduct technical feasibility study in planning phase
   o Maintain open communication with IT department
   Risk: Performance Issues with Large Media Files
   • Impact: Medium - Poor user experience
   • Likelihood: Low
   • Mitigation:
   o Implement CDN for content delivery
   o Use progressive video loading
   Risk: Security Vulnerabilities
   • Impact: High - Data breach, compliance violations
   • Likelihood: Low
   • Mitigation:
   o Follow OWASP security best practices
   o Regular security audits and penetration testing
   7.2 Adoption Risks
   Risk: Faculty Resistance to New System
   • Impact: High - Low adoption, underutilization
   • Likelihood: High
   • Mitigation:
   o Involve faculty in design process
   o Provide comprehensive training with incentives
   o Start with early adopters as champions
   Risk: Low Student Awareness
   • Impact: Medium - Underutilization
   • Likelihood: Medium
   • Mitigation:
   o Marketing campaign before launch
   o Mandatory orientation for BDD students
   o Visible promotion on campus
   7.3 Project Management Risks
   Risk: Timeline Delays
   • Impact: Medium - Budget overruns, missed launch date
   • Likelihood: Medium
   • Mitigation:
   o Use agile methodology with 2-week sprints
   o Build buffer time into schedule (10%)
   o Weekly progress reviews
   o Clear milestone tracking
